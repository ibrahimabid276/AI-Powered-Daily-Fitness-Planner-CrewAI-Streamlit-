4.  **Alternating Lunges:** (3 sets of 6-8 repetitions per leg)
    *   Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
    *   Step forward with one leg, lowering your hips until both knees are bent at approximately a 90-degree angle. Ensure your front knee is over your ankle, and your back knee hovers above the ground.
    *   Push off with your front foot to return to the starting position. Alternate legs.
    *   *Rest 30 seconds between sets.*

**Part 3: Cool-down (3 minutes)**
*   **Purpose:** Gradually lower your heart rate, improve flexibility, and signal your body to relax.
*   **Instructions:** Hold each stretch gently for 30 seconds. Breathe deeply and do not bounce.

1.  **Quad Stretch:** (30 seconds per leg)
    *   Stand tall, hold onto a wall or chair for balance. Grab your right foot with your right hand and gently pull your heel towards your glutes. Keep your knees together. Feel the stretch in the front of your thigh. Switch legs.
2.  **Hamstring Stretch:** (30 seconds per leg)
    *   Sit on the floor with one leg extended straight out and the other bent with your foot towards your inner thigh. Lean forward from your hips, reaching towards your extended foot until you feel a gentle stretch in the back of your thigh. Switch legs.
3.  **Overhead Triceps Stretch:** (30 seconds per arm)
    *   Raise one arm straight up, then bend your elbow so your hand touches your upper back. Use your other hand to gently push down on the bent elbow, deepening the stretch. Switch arms.
4.  **Chest Stretch:** (30 seconds)
    *   Stand in a doorway or near a wall. Place your forearm against the frame/wall, with your elbow at shoulder height. Gently lean forward until you feel a stretch across your chest and front of your shoulder. Repeat on the other side if using a wall; if using a doorway, you can do both arms at once.
5.  **Calf Stretch:** (30 seconds per leg)
    *   Stand facing a wall, place your hands on the wall. Step one foot back, keeping that leg straight and heel on the ground. Lean forward, feeling the stretch in the back of your lower leg. Switch legs.
6.  **Child's Pose:** (30 seconds)
    *   Kneel on the floor, sit your hips back towards your heels, and extend your arms forward, resting your forehead on the floor. Breathe deeply and relax your entire body.

*   **Form Over Speed:** Focus on doing each exercise correctly rather than doing it quickly.
*   **Hydration is Key:** Drink water before, during, and after your workout **to help your body function and recover properly.**
*   **Fuel Your Body for Recovery:** **After your workout, aim for a balanced snack or meal with some protein to help your muscles repair and grow, and carbohydrates to replenish energy.**
*   **Prioritize Sleep:** **Adequate sleep (8-10 hours for your age) is crucial for muscle repair, growth, and overall well-being. Your body recovers most effectively when you're sleeping.**
*   **Active Recovery on Rest Days:** **On days you're not doing this workout, consider light activities like walking, gentle cycling, or more stretching. This can help with blood flow and reduce muscle soreness.**
